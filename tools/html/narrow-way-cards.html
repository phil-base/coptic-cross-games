The Narrow Way — Coptic Cross Games — Situation Cards — Page 1 of 5

Situation
The Gossip Circle
Your friends are gossiping about someone from church. They turn to you expectantly, waiting for you to add something.
Broad Way: Join in. It feels good to belong. (+0 Virtue)
Narrow Way: Change the subject or gently say you'd rather not. (Draw a Trial)
Situation
The Easy Excuse
You promised to visit a sick relative this weekend, but a much more exciting invitation came up. No one will know if you don't go.
Broad Way: Skip the visit. You'll go another time. (+0 Virtue)
Narrow Way: Keep your promise. The sick relative needs you. (Draw a Trial)
Situation
The Fasting Challenge
It's a fasting period. Your friends who don't fast are ordering your favorite food. They tease you for fasting.
Broad Way: Break the fast. No one from church is watching. (+0 Virtue)
Narrow Way: Keep the fast cheerfully, without judging your friends. (Draw a Trial)
Situation
The Unfair Teacher
A teacher grades you unfairly. You're angry. Your friends say you should complain loudly and make a scene to embarrass the teacher.
Broad Way: Rage publicly. Make sure everyone knows you were wronged. (+0 Virtue)
Narrow Way: Speak to the teacher privately, calmly, with respect. (Draw a Trial)
Situation
The Midnight Scroll
It's late. You planned to pray before bed, but your phone is right there. Just five more minutes of scrolling...
Broad Way: Keep scrolling. You'll pray tomorrow. (+0 Virtue)
Narrow Way: Put the phone down. Open the Agpeya. (Draw a Trial)
Situation
The Popular Lie
Everyone believes something about a classmate that isn't true. You know the truth, but correcting them would make you unpopular.
Broad Way: Stay quiet. It's not your problem. (+0 Virtue)
Narrow Way: Speak the truth kindly, even if it costs you. (Draw a Trial)
Situation
The Grudge
Someone hurt you months ago and never apologized. You see them at church every week. The resentment is still there.
Broad Way: Avoid them. Hold onto your right to be angry. (+0 Virtue)
Narrow Way: Begin to forgive, even without an apology. (Draw a Trial)
Situation
The Secret Sin
You've been struggling with something privately. Your confession father asks how you're doing. You could say "fine" and move on.
Broad Way: Say "I'm fine." Keep it hidden. (+0 Virtue)
Narrow Way: Be honest. Open your heart in confession. (Draw a Trial)
Situation
The Comparison Trap
You see a friend's perfect life on social media. Envy rises. You start composing a post to make your life look equally impressive.
Broad Way: Post something exaggerated. Compete. (+0 Virtue)
Narrow Way: Close the app. Thank God for what you have. (Draw a Trial)

The Narrow Way — Situation Cards — Page 2 of 5

Situation
The Lazy Sunday
You're exhausted. The liturgy starts early. Your bed is warm. Missing one Sunday won't matter... right?
Broad Way: Sleep in. You deserve rest. (+0 Virtue)
Narrow Way: Get up. The Body and Blood of Christ are worth the effort. (Draw a Trial)
Situation
The Cheating Opportunity
During an exam, you can see another student's answers. The teacher isn't looking. You're struggling with this question.
Broad Way: Look. No one will ever know. (+0 Virtue)
Narrow Way: Look away. Accept whatever grade you earn honestly. (Draw a Trial)
Situation
The Generous Moment
You have money saved for something you want. A family at church is struggling. You could help, but no one is asking you to.
Broad Way: Keep your money. Someone else will help them. (+0 Virtue)
Narrow Way: Give quietly, without telling anyone. (Draw a Trial)
Situation
The Harsh Word
Your sibling said something annoying. A cutting response rises to your lips. You know exactly what to say to hurt them.
Broad Way: Say it. They started it. (+0 Virtue)
Narrow Way: Swallow the words. Respond gently or stay silent. (Draw a Trial)
Situation
The Mockery
Someone at school mocks your faith. "You still believe in that?" Others laugh. You feel the heat of shame.
Broad Way: Downplay your faith. Laugh along. (+0 Virtue)
Narrow Way: Stand calmly in your faith. You don't need to argue. (Draw a Trial)
Situation
The Boring Service
You're serving in Sunday school but you're bored of it. A friend invites you to quit and do something more fun on Sundays.
Broad Way: Quit. It's not rewarding anymore. (+0 Virtue)
Narrow Way: Persevere. Ask God to renew your love for service. (Draw a Trial)
Situation
The Prideful Win
You succeeded at something publicly. Pride swells. You want everyone to know how good you are.
Broad Way: Boast. Post about it. Accept all the praise. (+0 Virtue)
Narrow Way: Thank God quietly. Deflect the praise. (Draw a Trial)
Situation
The Lonely Outsider
A new person at youth group sits alone. Your group is comfortable. Including them would change the dynamic.
Broad Way: Stay comfortable. They'll figure it out. (+0 Virtue)
Narrow Way: Walk over. Introduce yourself. Welcome Christ in the stranger. (Draw a Trial)
Situation
The Angry Prayer
Something terrible happened. You're angry at God. You don't feel like praying. Silence feels easier than honesty.
Broad Way: Stop praying. God doesn't seem to listen anyway. (+0 Virtue)
Narrow Way: Pray honestly, even in anger. God can handle your honesty. (Draw a Trial)

The Narrow Way — Situation Cards 19-20 + Trial Cards 1-7 — Page 3 of 5

Situation
The Apology Owed
You wronged someone. You know you should apologize but your pride says they deserved what you did.
Broad Way: Justify yourself. They had it coming. (+0 Virtue)
Narrow Way: Humble yourself. Apologize sincerely. (Draw a Trial)
Situation
The Unseen Kindness
You have a chance to do something kind for someone who will never know it was you. No recognition. No thanks.
Broad Way: Why bother if no one will know? (+0 Virtue)
Narrow Way: Do it secretly. "Your Father who sees in secret will reward you." (Draw a Trial)
Trial

People laugh at your choice. "Why are you being so serious?" The temptation to give up is real.
Pass: +3 Virtue — Threshold: 3+
Trial
Loneliness
The narrow way is lonely. Your friends chose the broad way. You stand alone.
Pass: +3 Virtue — Threshold: 3+
Trial
Doubt
"Is this really worth it?" The voice of doubt whispers that virtue is pointless and no one cares.
Pass: +4 Virtue — Threshold: 4+
Trial
Exhaustion
You're tired. Doing the right thing takes energy you barely have. The easy path beckons.
Pass: +2 Virtue — Threshold: 2+
Trial
Temptation
The thing you're resisting looks so good. Just this once. No one will know.
Pass: +4 Virtue — Threshold: 4+

The Narrow Way — Trial Cards 8-16 — Page 4 of 5

Trial
Grace
In your moment of weakness, you remember a verse, a saint's example, or a word from your confession father. Strength comes.
Automatic Pass: +5 Virtue
Trial
Anger
The injustice of it burns. You did the right thing and got punished for it. Rage threatens to swallow your peace.
Pass: +4 Virtue — Threshold: 4+
Trial
Misunderstanding
People misread your motives. They think you're being self-righteous when you're just trying to do right.
Pass: +3 Virtue — Threshold: 3+
Trial
Delayed Reward
You did the right thing but nothing good happened. No praise, no result, no change. Was it worth it?
Pass: +3 Virtue — Threshold: 3+
Trial
Humiliation
Your faithfulness made you look foolish in front of people whose opinion matters to you.
Pass: +5 Virtue — Threshold: 5+
Trial
Persistence
This is the third time you've had to make this same hard choice. Will you keep choosing the narrow way?
Pass: +4 Virtue — Threshold: 4+
Trial
Fall
You stumbled. You chose the narrow way but your strength failed. Lose all Virtue earned this round. But remember: Abba Sisoes says, "Get up again."
Lose all Virtue from this round. But you may try again next round.
Trial
Peace
Despite the cost, a deep peace settles in your heart. You know you did what was right. God is near.
Automatic Pass: +5 Virtue
Trial
Companion
Someone else chose the narrow way with you. You're not alone. Courage rises.
Automatic Pass: +4 Virtue. Another player also gains +2.

The Narrow Way — Trial Cards 17-20 + Card Backs — Page 5 of 5

Pass: +5 Virtue — Threshold: 5+
Trial
Comfort Zone
The narrow way takes you outside everything familiar. New territory. No map. Just trust.
Pass: +3 Virtue — Threshold: 3+
Trial
Pride
You chose well and now you feel superior to those who didn't. The narrow way just became a pedestal.
Lose 2 Virtue. Pride turns even good choices into poison.

Joy
