About This Game

We Believe turns the Nicene Creed into a hands-on learning experience. Players race to arrange the Creed's phrases in correct order — or work together to build it as a team, pausing to discuss what each phrase means. Every word of the Creed was chosen to defend the truth about who God is.

Formation Goal: Understanding the Creed not as a formula to recite but as the faith the Church fought and suffered to preserve.

Components

  • 32 Phrase Cards — the complete Nicene Creed in natural phrase segments, colour-coded by article (Father, Son, Spirit, Church, Final)
  • 13 Challenge Cards — questions about meaning, history, and the saints who defended the faith
  • 1 Reference Sheet — the full Creed with annotations

Colour Guide

  • Blue — God the Father (cards 1-4)
  • Red — God the Son (cards 5-25)
  • Green — God the Holy Spirit (cards 26-28)
  • Gold — The Church (cards 29-30)
  • Purple — Final Declarations (cards 31-32)

Game Mode 1: Race

  1. Give each player a complete set of Phrase Cards (you will need to print multiple copies).
  2. Shuffle each player's cards face-down.
  3. On "Go!" all players flip their cards and race to arrange them in the correct order.
  4. First player to complete the Creed in order wins!
  5. Verify the winner's order against the Reference Sheet.

Game Mode 2: Teaching (Recommended)

  1. Use one set of Phrase Cards. Shuffle and deal evenly among all players.
  2. Place card 1 ("We believe in one God") face-up on the table.
  3. Players take turns placing the next phrase in sequence. If you have it, play it. If not, say "pass."
  4. After each card is placed, the leader (or another player) reads the phrase aloud. Use the Leader Guide to discuss: What does this phrase mean? Why was it included?
  5. Continue until the entire Creed is assembled.

Challenge Cards (Optional)

Shuffle the Challenge Cards into a separate pile. After completing the Creed (or during Teaching Mode), draw Challenge Cards for bonus discussion:

  • If a player can answer the question, they keep the card as a bonus point.
  • If no one knows, read the hint together and discuss. No penalty.
  • Challenge Cards work best with older children (ages 10+) and youth groups.

Tips for Teachers

  • For younger children (ages 7-8), use only the colour bands to group phrases by article first, then order within each group.
  • Repeat weekly during a Creed study unit — children will memorise the Creed naturally through play.
  • Connect to the Liturgy: "This is what we stand and say together every Sunday."
  • On St. Athanasius's feast day, tell the story of how he defended these words.
