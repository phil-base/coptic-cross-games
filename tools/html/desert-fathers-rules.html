Desert Fathers Wisdom

A discussion game for adults — Ages 18+ — 3-8 Players — 30 Minutes

Age Range
18+
Players
3-8
Play Time
30 minutes
Difficulty
Easy

About This Game

The desert fathers and mothers are the spiritual ancestors of the Coptic Church. Their sayings — brief, often paradoxical, always penetrating — have guided souls toward God for seventeen centuries. Desert Fathers Wisdom brings these sayings to the table for honest, shared reflection.

Formation Goal: Cultivating humility, self-awareness, and watchfulness — the core of the desert tradition.

Components

  • 40 Saying Cards — each containing a saying with attribution
  • Rules (this sheet)
  • Leader Guide — historical context and discussion prompts
  • Reflection Sheet — for personal journaling after the session

Setup

  1. Print the saying cards on cardstock and cut along borders.
  2. Shuffle all 40 cards and place them face-down in a stack.
  3. Sit in a circle. Choose one person to facilitate (or take turns).
  4. Open with a brief prayer: "Lord, open our hearts to receive Your wisdom through the words of the fathers. Amen."

How to Play

  1. The facilitator draws the top card and reads the saying aloud. Read it slowly.
  2. Pause in silence for 15-30 seconds. Let the saying settle.
  3. Going around the circle, each person shares a brief response. Suggested prompts:
    • "What strikes you about this saying?"
    • "What does this challenge in your own life?"
    • "What do you not understand about this saying?"
  4. After everyone has shared, the facilitator may read the context from the Leader Guide.
  5. Draw the next card. Continue for as many rounds as time allows (typically 5-8 sayings in 30 minutes).

Guidelines for Discussion

  • There are no points. There is no winner. The conversation is the game.
  • Listen more than you speak. The desert fathers valued silence.
  • Speak about yourself, not others. "This challenges me because..." not "People should..."
  • It is fine to be confused. Many sayings are paradoxical. Sit with the discomfort.
  • Do not argue. Share perspectives, but do not debate. Receive what others say.

Closing

After the final saying, close with a moment of silence and a prayer. Distribute the Reflection Sheet for those who wish to continue processing privately.

Variants

  • Personal meditation: Use the cards alone as daily meditation prompts. Draw one card per day.
  • Written response: Instead of speaking, have each person write their response first, then share.
  • Themed session: Sort cards by theme (humility, prayer, judgement, repentance) and use one theme per session.
