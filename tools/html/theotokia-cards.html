Theotokia — Coptic Cross Games — Print on cardstock, cut along borders — Page 1 of 6 (Title Cards 1-9)

Title
👑
Theotokos (Mother of God)
The Council of Ephesus (431 AD) affirmed this title to defend the truth that the One born of Mary is truly God. She did not bear a mere man — she bore the incarnate Word.
Why is it important that Mary is called "Mother of God" and not just "mother of Jesus"?
Title
📦
The Ark of the Covenant
As the Ark carried the tablets of the Law, the manna, and Aaron's rod, so Mary carried within her the Lawgiver Himself, the Bread of Life, and the eternal High Priest.
What does it mean that Mary carried God within her, as the Ark carried holy things?
Title
🔥
The Burning Bush
Moses saw a bush that burned with fire yet was not consumed. The Church Fathers teach that Mary held the fire of divinity within her womb yet was not consumed by it.
How does the burning bush help us understand the mystery of the Incarnation?
Title
🚪
The Ladder of Jacob
Jacob saw a ladder connecting heaven and earth, with angels ascending and descending. Through Mary, heaven and earth were joined — God descended to become man.
In what way did Mary become a bridge between heaven and earth?
Title
🚪
The Gate of Heaven
Ezekiel saw a gate shut and sealed, through which only the Lord would pass. Mary is the sealed gate through whom God entered the world, her virginity preserved.
What does the "sealed gate" teach us about Mary's perpetual virginity?
Title
👑
Queen of All (Lady of Us All)
As the mother of the King of Kings, Mary holds a place of honour above all creation. She intercedes for us with a mother's love and a queen's authority before her Son.
How is Mary's queenship different from earthly power?
Title
✨
The Ever-Virgin
The Coptic Church confesses that Mary remained a virgin before, during, and after the birth of Christ. Her virginity is a sign of total consecration to God.
Why does the Church emphasise Mary's perpetual virginity?
Title
🌈
The Second Heaven
If heaven is the dwelling place of God, then Mary's womb became a second heaven — for the God whom the heavens cannot contain chose to dwell within her.
What does it mean that God chose to dwell in a human person?
Title
🏆
The Golden Censer
The golden censer held the incense that rose before God. Mary carried within her the One whose sacrifice is a sweet aroma to the Father — the offering that saves the world.
How is Mary like a vessel that carries something precious to God?

Theotokia — Coptic Cross Games — Page 2 of 6 (Title Card 10 + Feast Cards 1-8)

Title
🍇
The Vine of the True Vine
Christ called Himself the True Vine. Mary is the vine from which this Vine took flesh. Through her, the fruit of salvation was given to the world.
How does Mary's role connect to Christ as the "True Vine"?
Feast
💌
The Annunciation (29 Baramhat)
The angel Gabriel brought God's invitation, and Mary said "yes." Her obedience reversed Eve's disobedience. In that moment, the Word became flesh.
What can we learn from Mary's willingness to say "yes" to God's plan?
Feast
🌟
The Nativity of the Theotokos (1 Bashans)
The birth of Mary was the dawn before the sunrise. God prepared her from birth to be the vessel of the Incarnation — the one through whom salvation would enter the world.
Why does the Church celebrate the birth of St. Mary?
Feast
⛪
Her Entry into the Temple (3 Kiahk)
As a young child, Mary was presented to the Temple, where she grew in prayer and devotion. She was raised in God's house, preparing to become the living temple of God.
How does Mary's life in the Temple inspire our own devotion?
Feast
⭐
The Nativity of Christ (29 Kiahk)
In a humble manger, Mary brought forth the Saviour of the world. The God of the universe chose to enter creation through the womb of a poor virgin in Bethlehem.
What does it mean that God chose humility as His way into the world?
Feast
🌴
The Flight to Egypt (24 Bashans)
Mary and Joseph fled to Egypt to protect the child Jesus from Herod. The Holy Family's journey blessed the land of Egypt, fulfilling Isaiah's prophecy: "Blessed be Egypt My people."
How does the Holy Family's flight remind us that God is with us in hardship?
Feast
🍷
The Wedding at Cana (13 Tobi)
Mary noticed the need before anyone asked. She brought it to her Son and told the servants, "Whatever He says to you, do it." Her intercession led to Christ's first miracle.
What does the Wedding at Cana teach us about asking St. Mary to intercede?
Feast
✝
At the Cross (Good Friday)
Mary stood at the foot of the Cross, watching her Son suffer and die. Simeon's prophecy was fulfilled: a sword pierced her soul. Her faithfulness did not waver even in the darkest hour.
What does Mary's presence at the Cross teach us about faithfulness in suffering?

Theotokia — Coptic Cross Games — Page 3 of 6 (Feast Cards 9-10 + Scripture Cards 1-7)

Feast
🌞
The Assumption (16 Mesori)
The Coptic Church commemorates Mary's departure from this world and her body being taken up to heaven. She who bore the Author of Life did not see corruption in death.
What does the Assumption teach us about the hope of resurrection?
Feast
✨
Apparition at Zeitoun (2 April 1968)
For years, St. Mary appeared above the domes of a church in Zeitoun, Cairo, witnessed by millions of Christians and Muslims alike. She came as a mother comforting her children in a time of trial.
Why do you think St. Mary appeared in Egypt, and what does this tell us about her care?
Feast
🕊
Presentation of Christ in the Temple (8 Amshir)
Mary and Joseph brought the infant Jesus to the Temple, where Simeon received Him and prophesied. Mary's obedience to the Law showed her humility before God.
What does it mean that Mary submitted to God's commands even though she carried God Himself?
Scripture
📖
Luke 1:26-38 — The Annunciation
"Behold the handmaid of the Lord; let it be to me according to your word." Mary's response to the angel is the model of faithful obedience — complete surrender to God's will.
When has God asked something difficult of you? How did you respond?
Scripture
🎶
Luke 1:46-55 — The Magnificat
"My soul magnifies the Lord." Mary's hymn of praise reveals a heart overflowing with gratitude. She saw herself as lowly, yet God had done great things through her.
What are the "great things" God has done in your life that you can praise Him for?
Scripture
⭐
Luke 2:6-7 — The Birth of Christ
"She wrapped Him in swaddling cloths and laid Him in a manger." The Creator of the universe was wrapped in cloth by human hands — the hands of His mother.
What does the simplicity of Christ's birth tell us about God's character?
Scripture
💜
Luke 2:19 — "Mary kept all these things"
"Mary kept all these things, pondering them in her heart." She did not rush to speak or boast. She held God's mysteries in silent contemplation — a model of inner prayer.
How can we practise "pondering in our hearts" like Mary?
Scripture
🗡
Luke 2:34-35 — Simeon's Prophecy
"A sword will pierce your own soul also." Simeon warned Mary that her path would include suffering. Her closeness to Christ meant sharing in His pain as well as His glory.
Why does loving deeply sometimes mean suffering deeply?

Theotokia — Coptic Cross Games — Page 4 of 6 (Scripture Cards 8-10 + Hymn Cards 1-6)

Scripture
🍷
John 2:1-5 — The Wedding at Cana
"Whatever He says to you, do it." Mary's words to the servants at Cana are her words to all of us. She always points us toward Christ, never toward herself.
How does Mary's instruction at Cana guide our relationship with Christ?
Scripture
✝
John 19:25-27 — "Behold your mother"
From the Cross, Jesus entrusted His mother to the beloved disciple and the disciple to His mother. The Church understands this as Christ giving Mary as mother to all believers.
What does it mean to you personally that Mary is given as a mother to the Church?
Scripture
🔥
Acts 1:14 — Mary in the Upper Room
After the Ascension, Mary was present with the disciples in prayer, awaiting the Holy Spirit. She who received the Spirit at the Annunciation was there when the Spirit came upon the Church.
Why is it significant that Mary was with the Apostles at Pentecost?
Scripture
🌞
Revelation 12:1 — The Woman Clothed with the Sun
"A great sign appeared in heaven: a woman clothed with the sun, with the moon under her feet." The Church Fathers see in this image a vision of St. Mary's glory and the Church she represents.
How does this heavenly image reflect the honour given to St. Mary?
Scripture
🐍
Genesis 3:15 — The Protoevangelium
"Her seed shall crush the serpent's head." From the very beginning, God promised that through a woman's offspring, evil would be defeated. Mary is the fulfilment of this first gospel promise.
How does Genesis 3:15 connect Mary to God's plan of salvation from the very start?
Hymn
🎵
Theotokia of Sunday
"O Mary, the fair dove, who bore for us God the Word..." The Sunday Theotokia celebrates the Resurrection and Mary's role in the Incarnation that made our salvation possible.
How does the Resurrection give meaning to Mary's "yes" at the Annunciation?
Hymn
🎵
Theotokia of Monday
"You are the golden censer, bearing the blessed coal of fire..." Monday's Theotokia uses the image of the censer to show how Mary carried Christ, the fire of divinity, without being consumed.
What does the image of the golden censer teach about carrying Christ in our lives?

Theotokia — Coptic Cross Games — Page 5 of 6 (Hymn Cards 7-10 + Card Backs begin)

Hymn
🔥
Theotokia of Tuesday
"The burning bush that Moses saw in the wilderness is a type of the Theotokos..." Tuesday's Theotokia draws on Exodus to declare that Mary bore the fire of God yet remained unconsumed.
Why do you think the Church uses Old Testament images to honour Mary?
Hymn
📦
Theotokia of Wednesday
"The Ark overlaid with gold on every side is the Ever-Virgin..." Wednesday's Theotokia connects the Ark of the Covenant to Mary, who carried the Word of God within her.
How does the Ark imagery deepen your understanding of Mary's holiness?
Hymn
🎵
Theotokia of Thursday
"Mary, the Mother of God, the faithful advocate..." Thursday's Theotokia highlights Mary's role as intercessor — one who faithfully brings our prayers before her Son.
What does it mean to have Mary as an "advocate" before Christ?
Hymn
🌈
Theotokia of Friday
"A new heaven has been made, more honourable than the first..." Friday's Theotokia calls Mary a new heaven because God dwelt in her. She is more glorious than the heavens themselves.
Why does the Church call Mary "more honourable than the Cherubim"?
Hymn
🎵
Theotokia of Saturday
"O Virgin Mary, the Mother of God, the faithful intercessor of the human race..." Saturday's Theotokia gathers the week's themes, confessing Mary as mother and intercessor for all humanity.
How does praying the Theotokia each day shape our relationship with St. Mary?
Hymn
📖
Kiahk Psalmody
"Blessed are you among women, and blessed is the fruit of your womb..." The month of Kiahk overflows with praise to St. Mary, preparing our hearts for the Nativity of Christ.
Why does the Church dedicate an entire month to praising St. Mary before Christmas?
Hymn
🎶
The Seven-and-Four
"All generations shall call me blessed, for He who is mighty has done great things for me." This ancient hymn echoes the Magnificat, reminding us that honouring Mary fulfils Scripture itself.
How does honouring Mary fulfil her own prophecy in Luke 1:48?
Hymn
🙏
Lobsh of the Theotokos
"We hymn you, O Theotokos, the precious stone of chastity..." The Lobsh is a doxology of deep reverence, praising Mary's purity, her role in salvation, and her ceaseless intercession.
What does it mean to "hymn" or praise someone, and why do we do this for St. Mary?
