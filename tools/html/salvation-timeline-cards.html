Creation & Patriarchs
Creation of the World
In the Beginning
God creates heaven and earth in six days and rests on the seventh. He makes humanity in His own image.
Genesis 1–2
Creation & Patriarchs
The Fall of Adam and Eve
In the Beginning
Adam and Eve disobey God and eat the forbidden fruit. Sin and death enter the world, but God promises a Savior.
Genesis 3
Creation & Patriarchs
Cain and Abel
In the Beginning
Abel offers a pleasing sacrifice to God. Cain, jealous, murders his brother — the first sin of violence.
Genesis 4:1–16
Creation & Patriarchs
Noah and the Great Flood
c. 3000 BC
God sends a flood to cleanse the earth. Noah's family and the animals are saved in the ark. God sets a rainbow as His covenant sign.
Genesis 6–9
Creation & Patriarchs
The Tower of Babel
After the Flood
Humanity builds a tower to reach heaven through their own power. God confuses their languages and scatters them.
Genesis 11:1–9
Creation & Patriarchs
God Calls Abraham
c. 2000 BC
God calls Abram to leave his homeland and promises to make him the father of a great nation through whom all peoples will be blessed.
Genesis 12:1–3
Creation & Patriarchs
The Binding of Isaac
c. 2000 BC
God tests Abraham by asking him to sacrifice Isaac. Abraham obeys, and God provides a ram — prefiguring Christ's sacrifice on the Cross.
Genesis 22:1–14
Creation & Patriarchs
Jacob's Ladder
c. 1900 BC
Jacob dreams of a ladder reaching heaven with angels ascending and descending. God renews the covenant promise. The Fathers see this as an image of the Theotokos.
Genesis 28:10–17
Creation & Patriarchs
Joseph Sold into Egypt
c. 1880 BC
Joseph's brothers sell him into slavery, but God turns evil to good. Joseph rises to save Egypt and his family from famine.
Genesis 37; 41–45

Creation & Patriarchs
Israel in Egypt
c. 1870 BC
Jacob's family settles in Egypt. Over centuries they multiply into a great nation, but a new Pharaoh enslaves them.
Genesis 46; Exodus 1
Exodus & Conquest
The Birth of Moses
c. 1530 BC
Pharaoh orders all Hebrew boys killed. Moses' mother places him in a basket on the Nile. Pharaoh's daughter rescues him.
Exodus 2:1–10
Exodus & Conquest
The Burning Bush
c. 1450 BC
God appears to Moses in a bush that burns but is not consumed and reveals His name: I AM WHO I AM. The Fathers see the bush as an image of the Theotokos.
Exodus 3:1–15
Exodus & Conquest
The Ten Plagues
c. 1450 BC
God sends ten plagues on Egypt to free His people. The final plague — the death of the firstborn — brings Pharaoh to release Israel.
Exodus 7–11
Exodus & Conquest
The First Passover
c. 1450 BC
Israel marks their doorposts with lamb's blood and the angel of death passes over. The Passover lamb prefigures Christ, the Lamb of God.
Exodus 12
Exodus & Conquest
Crossing the Red Sea
c. 1450 BC
God parts the Red Sea and Israel walks through on dry ground. Pharaoh's army is destroyed. The Church Fathers see this as a type of baptism.
Exodus 14
Exodus & Conquest
Manna from Heaven
c. 1450 BC
God feeds Israel with bread from heaven in the wilderness. Jesus later reveals Himself as the true Bread of Life.
Exodus 16; John 6:31–35
Exodus & Conquest
The Ten Commandments
c. 1450 BC
God gives Moses the Law on Mount Sinai — ten commandments written by God's own finger on tablets of stone.
Exodus 19–20
Exodus & Conquest
The Golden Calf
c. 1450 BC
While Moses is on Sinai, the people make a golden idol. Moses intercedes for them. God punishes but does not destroy His people.
Exodus 32

Exodus & Conquest
The Tabernacle Built
c. 1450 BC
God commands Moses to build a tabernacle — a dwelling place where He will meet His people. The Ark of the Covenant holds the tablets of the Law.
Exodus 25–27; 40
Exodus & Conquest
The Bronze Serpent
c. 1410 BC
Poisonous serpents afflict Israel. Moses raises a bronze serpent on a pole, and all who look upon it are healed — a type of the Cross.
Numbers 21:4–9; John 3:14
Exodus & Conquest
Crossing the Jordan
c. 1410 BC
Joshua leads Israel across the Jordan into the Promised Land. The waters part as the priests carry the Ark forward.
Joshua 3–4
Exodus & Conquest
The Fall of Jericho
c. 1400 BC
Israel marches around Jericho for seven days. On the seventh day they shout, and the walls fall. Victory comes through faith, not force.
Joshua 6
Judges & Kings
Deborah Judges Israel
c. 1200 BC
The prophetess Deborah leads Israel to victory over Sisera's army. God raises up women to deliver His people.
Judges 4–5
Judges & Kings
Gideon's 300 Warriors
c. 1150 BC
God reduces Gideon's army from 32,000 to 300 so that Israel will know the victory is God's, not theirs.
Judges 7
Judges & Kings
Samson's Strength
c. 1100 BC
God gives Samson supernatural strength to defend Israel. His final act of self-sacrifice defeats the Philistines.
Judges 13–16
Judges & Kings
Ruth's Faithfulness
c. 1100 BC
Ruth the Moabite clings to her mother-in-law Naomi and follows her to Israel. She becomes an ancestor of King David and of Christ.
Ruth 1–4
Judges & Kings
Hannah's Prayer
c. 1100 BC
Hannah prays with tears for a child and vows to dedicate him to God. God grants her Samuel, who becomes a great prophet.
1 Samuel 1

Judges & Kings
Samuel Anoints Saul
c. 1050 BC
Israel demands a king like the other nations. God gives them Saul, but warns that earthly kings will fail them.
1 Samuel 8–10
Judges & Kings
David and Goliath
c. 1025 BC
The young shepherd David defeats the giant Goliath with a sling and a stone, trusting not in armor but in the name of the Lord.
1 Samuel 17
Judges & Kings
David Becomes King
c. 1010 BC
David is anointed king over all Israel and makes Jerusalem his capital. God promises that David's throne will last forever.
2 Samuel 5–7
Judges & Kings
The Psalms of David
c. 1000 BC
David writes psalms of praise, repentance, and prophecy. The Coptic Church prays the Psalms daily in the Agpeya and before every liturgical reading.
Psalms
Judges & Kings
Solomon Builds the Temple
c. 960 BC
Solomon builds the Temple in Jerusalem, and the glory of the Lord fills it. God now has a permanent dwelling among His people.
1 Kings 6–8
Judges & Kings
The Kingdom Divides
c. 930 BC
After Solomon's death, the kingdom splits in two: Israel in the north and Judah in the south. Both will struggle with idolatry.
1 Kings 12
Prophets & Exile
Elijah on Mount Carmel
c. 860 BC
Elijah challenges the prophets of Baal. Fire from heaven consumes his sacrifice, proving the Lord alone is God.
1 Kings 18
Prophets & Exile
Elijah Taken Up
c. 850 BC
Elijah is taken to heaven in a chariot of fire. His mantle falls to Elisha, who receives a double portion of his spirit.
2 Kings 2:1–14
Prophets & Exile
Jonah and the Great Fish
c. 780 BC
Jonah flees God's command and is swallowed by a great fish for three days. Jesus points to Jonah as a sign of His own burial and resurrection.
Jonah 1–2; Matthew 12:40

Prophets & Exile
Isaiah's Vision
c. 740 BC
Isaiah sees the Lord enthroned, with seraphim crying “Holy, Holy, Holy!” He is cleansed and called to prophesy the coming Messiah.
Isaiah 6; 7:14; 53
Prophets & Exile
The Fall of the Northern Kingdom
722 BC
Assyria conquers Israel. The ten northern tribes are scattered. Idolatry and unfaithfulness bring God's judgment.
2 Kings 17
Prophets & Exile
The Three Holy Youth
c. 600 BC
Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego refuse to worship the golden image and are cast into the fiery furnace. A fourth figure appears with them.
Daniel 3
Prophets & Exile
The Fall of Jerusalem
586 BC
Babylon destroys the Temple and takes Judah into exile. The prophets had warned for generations, but the people did not repent.
2 Kings 25; Jeremiah 52
Prophets & Exile
Daniel in the Lions' Den
c. 540 BC
Daniel prays to God despite the king's decree and is thrown into a den of lions. God sends an angel to shut the lions' mouths.
Daniel 6
Prophets & Exile
The Return from Exile
538 BC
King Cyrus of Persia allows the Jews to return to Jerusalem and rebuild the Temple. God keeps His promise to restore His people.
Ezra 1–3
Prophets & Exile
The Temple Rebuilt
516 BC
Under Zerubbabel, the second Temple is completed. The elders weep, remembering Solomon's Temple, but the prophets promise greater glory to come.
Ezra 6; Haggai 2:9
Prophets & Exile
Esther Saves Her People
c. 480 BC
Queen Esther risks her life to save the Jewish people from destruction. Her courage and fasting deliver an entire nation.
Esther 4–8
Prophets & Exile
The Holy Family in Egypt
c. 4 BC
Fulfilling Hosea's prophecy, the Holy Family flees to Egypt. The Coptic Church celebrates this journey that blessed the land of Egypt.
Matthew 2:13–15; Hosea 11:1

The Incarnation
The Annunciation
c. 5 BC
The Archangel Gabriel appears to the Virgin Mary and announces she will bear the Son of God. Mary says: “Let it be to me according to your word.”
Luke 1:26–38
The Incarnation
The Nativity of Christ
c. 4 BC
Jesus is born in Bethlehem. Angels announce His birth to shepherds. The Magi follow a star to worship the newborn King.
Luke 2:1–20; Matthew 2:1–12
The Incarnation
The Baptism of Christ
c. AD 27
John baptizes Jesus in the Jordan. The Holy Spirit descends like a dove and the Father declares: “This is My beloved Son.” The Coptic feast of Epiphany celebrates this theophany.
Matthew 3:13–17
The Incarnation
The Temptation in the Wilderness
c. AD 27
Jesus fasts forty days and defeats Satan's temptations with the Word of God. He shows us how to resist the evil one.
Matthew 4:1–11
The Incarnation
The Sermon on the Mount
c. AD 28
Jesus teaches the Beatitudes and the way of the Kingdom: love your enemies, pray in secret, do not worry, build your house on rock.
Matthew 5–7
The Incarnation
The Transfiguration
c. AD 30
Jesus reveals His divine glory on the mountain. Moses and Elijah appear with Him. The Father's voice speaks again: “Hear Him!”
Matthew 17:1–8
The Incarnation
The Raising of Lazarus
c. AD 30
Jesus raises Lazarus from the dead after four days in the tomb. “I am the resurrection and the life” — a foretaste of the general resurrection.
John 11:1–44
The Incarnation
Palm Sunday
c. AD 30
Jesus enters Jerusalem riding a donkey. Crowds wave palm branches and cry “Hosanna!” The humble King comes in peace.
Matthew 21:1–11
The Incarnation
The Last Supper
c. AD 30
Jesus shares the Passover meal with His disciples and institutes the Holy Eucharist: “This is My Body… This is My Blood.”
Matthew 26:17–30; Luke 22:14–20

The Incarnation
The Crucifixion
c. AD 30
Jesus is crucified on Golgotha, bearing the sins of the world. He forgives His enemies and commends His spirit to the Father. The Cross becomes the Tree of Life.
Matthew 27; John 19
The Incarnation
The Resurrection
c. AD 30
On the third day, Christ rises from the dead! The tomb is empty. Death is conquered. “Christ is risen from the dead; by death He has trampled down death.”
Matthew 28; John 20
The Incarnation
The Ascension
c. AD 30
Forty days after the Resurrection, Jesus ascends to heaven. He promises to send the Holy Spirit and to return in glory.
Acts 1:6–11
The Early Church
Pentecost
c. AD 30
The Holy Spirit descends on the apostles as tongues of fire. They preach boldly and three thousand are baptized. The Church is born.
Acts 2
The Early Church
The Stoning of St. Stephen
c. AD 34
Stephen, full of grace, becomes the first Christian martyr. He sees heaven open and forgives his killers, as Christ did on the Cross.
Acts 6–7
The Early Church
The Conversion of St. Paul
c. AD 35
Saul the persecutor meets the risen Christ on the road to Damascus and becomes Paul, the apostle to the nations.
Acts 9:1–19
The Early Church
St. Mark in Egypt
c. AD 49
St. Mark brings the Gospel to Alexandria and founds the Coptic Church. The faith takes root in Egypt and flourishes to this day.
Coptic Tradition
The Early Church
The Council of Jerusalem
c. AD 50
The apostles gather to decide: must Gentile believers follow the Jewish law? The Holy Spirit guides the Church to welcome all nations.
Acts 15
