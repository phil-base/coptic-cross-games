Miracles and Mercy — Coptic Cross Games — Print on cardstock — Page 1 of 4

1
Water into Wine
John 2:1-11
At a wedding in Cana, the wine ran out. Mary told Jesus, and He turned water into wine — His first miracle, revealing His glory.
Why did Jesus perform His first miracle at a wedding?
What does this teach about bringing our needs to God?
Young ones: What did Mary say to the servants?
2
Healing the Paralytic
John 5:1-15
A man lay by the pool of Bethesda for 38 years, waiting for healing. Jesus said, "Rise, take up your bed and walk." And he was healed.
Why did Jesus ask, "Do you want to be made well?"
What paralyzes us spiritually? What would Jesus say to us?
Young ones: How long did the man wait? Who healed him?
3
Feeding the Five Thousand
John 6:1-14
A boy offered five loaves and two fish. Jesus blessed them and fed five thousand people, with twelve baskets left over.
What does it mean that a child's small offering was enough?
How does this connect to the Eucharist?
Young ones: How many loaves? How many fish? Who gave them?
4
Walking on Water
Matthew 14:22-33
In a storm, Jesus walked on the sea to His disciples. Peter stepped out to meet Him but began to sink when he looked at the waves. Jesus caught him.
What made Peter sink? What makes us sink?
What does it mean that Jesus immediately reached out His hand?
Young ones: What did Peter do when he was afraid?
5
The Man Born Blind
John 9:1-41
Jesus put clay on a blind man's eyes and told him to wash in the Pool of Siloam. He washed, and he could see. The Pharisees were angry.
The man said, "One thing I know: I was blind, now I see." What is our testimony?
Why were the Pharisees blind when the blind man could see?
Young ones: What did Jesus put on the man's eyes?
6
Raising of Lazarus
John 11:1-44
Lazarus had been dead four days. Jesus wept at his tomb, then called out, "Lazarus, come forth!" And Lazarus came out, alive.
"Jesus wept." What does this tell us about God's heart?
Is anything too dead for God to bring back to life?
Young ones: How many days was Lazarus in the tomb?
7
The Transfiguration
Matthew 17:1-8
On a high mountain, Jesus's face shone like the sun and His clothes became white as light. Moses and Elijah appeared with Him. A voice said, "This is My beloved Son."
Why did Jesus reveal His glory to only three disciples?
What does it mean for us that Christ's humanity was full of divine light?
Young ones: What happened to Jesus's face? Who appeared with Him?
8
Calming the Storm
Mark 4:35-41
A great storm arose while Jesus slept in the boat. The disciples woke Him in terror. He said, "Peace, be still!" and the wind and waves obeyed.
What storms in your life need Christ's "Peace, be still"?
Why was Jesus sleeping? What does His peace teach us?
Young ones: What did Jesus say to the storm?
9
Healing the Leper
Mark 1:40-45
A leper knelt before Jesus: "If You are willing, You can make me clean." Jesus, moved with compassion, touched him and said, "I am willing. Be clean."
Jesus touched the untouchable. Who are the "untouchables" in our world?
What does "I am willing" tell us about God's heart toward sinners?
Young ones: What did the sick man say to Jesus? What did Jesus say back?

Miracles and Mercy — Page 2 of 4

10
The Widow's Son at Nain
Luke 7:11-17
Jesus met a funeral procession. A widow's only son had died. He touched the coffin and said, "Young man, arise." The dead man sat up and began to speak.
Jesus saw her grief and was moved. What does this teach about how God sees our sorrow?
Young ones: What happened when Jesus touched the coffin?
11
Healing the Woman with the Issue of Blood
Mark 5:25-34
A woman who had been bleeding for twelve years touched Jesus's garment in the crowd. She was healed instantly. Jesus said, "Your faith has made you well."
She reached out in desperation and hope. When have you reached out to God like that?
Young ones: How many years was the woman sick? What did she touch?
12
Healing Jairus's Daughter
Mark 5:21-43
Jairus begged Jesus to heal his daughter. Word came: she was dead. Jesus said, "Do not be afraid; only believe." He took her hand and said, "Little girl, arise."
"Do not be afraid; only believe." When is this hardest to hear?
Young ones: What did Jesus say to the little girl?
13
The Healing of the Ten Lepers
Luke 17:11-19
Ten lepers cried out for mercy. Jesus healed them all. Only one returned to give thanks — and he was a Samaritan.
All ten were healed, but only one gave thanks. Do we remember to thank God?
Young ones: How many were healed? How many said thank you?
14
The Miraculous Catch of Fish
Luke 5:1-11
After fishing all night and catching nothing, Simon Peter obeyed Jesus's word and let down his nets. They caught so many fish the nets began to break.
Peter obeyed even when it didn't make sense. When has obedience been hard for you?
Young ones: What happened when Peter obeyed Jesus?
15
Healing the Paralytic through the Roof
Mark 2:1-12
Four friends lowered a paralyzed man through the roof to reach Jesus. Jesus saw their faith and said, "Son, your sins are forgiven." Then He healed the man.
Jesus forgave sins first, then healed. What does this order teach us?
The man was healed because of his friends' faith. How does the faith of others carry us?
Young ones: How did the friends get the man to Jesus?
16
The Healing at the Pool of Bethesda
John 5:1-9
"Sir, I have no one to put me into the pool." For 38 years, the man waited for someone to help. Then Jesus came and healed him with a word.
Have you ever waited so long you stopped hoping? What does this miracle say about God's timing?
Young ones: How long had the man been waiting?
17
Casting Out the Legion
Mark 5:1-20
A man lived among the tombs, possessed by a legion of demons. Jesus freed him. The man begged to follow Jesus, but Jesus sent him home to tell what God had done.
Jesus sent the healed man to testify. How do we share what God has done for us?
Young ones: Where did the man live before Jesus helped him? Where did Jesus send him after?
18
The Withered Hand
Mark 3:1-6
In the synagogue on the Sabbath, Jesus healed a man with a withered hand. The Pharisees plotted against Him because He healed on the Sabbath.
Jesus asked, "Is it lawful to do good or to do evil on the Sabbath?" When do rules get in the way of mercy?
Young ones: What part of the man did Jesus heal?

Miracles and Mercy — Page 3 of 4

19
The Centurion's Servant
Matthew 8:5-13
A Roman centurion asked Jesus to heal his servant. "Just say the word, and my servant will be healed." Jesus marveled: "I have not found such great faith in Israel."
The centurion trusted Jesus's authority over distance and sickness. How deep is our trust?
Young ones: Did Jesus need to visit the servant? What did He do instead?
20
Healing the Deaf and Mute Man
Mark 7:31-37
Jesus put His fingers in the man's ears, touched his tongue, and said, "Ephphatha" — "Be opened." And the man could hear and speak.
What are we deaf to that God wants to open? What are we unable to say?
Young ones: What word did Jesus say? What does it mean?
21
The Coin in the Fish's Mouth
Matthew 17:24-27
When tax was due, Jesus told Peter to cast a hook into the sea. The first fish he caught had a coin in its mouth — exactly enough to pay the tax for both of them.
God provides in surprising ways. When has God provided for you unexpectedly?
Young ones: What was inside the fish's mouth?
22
Healing the Blind Bartimaeus
Mark 10:46-52
Bartimaeus cried out, "Son of David, have mercy on me!" People told him to be quiet, but he cried louder. Jesus stopped and called him. "What do you want?" "Lord, that I may see."
Bartimaeus refused to be silenced. What keeps us from crying out to God?
Young ones: What did Bartimaeus keep saying even when people told him to stop?
23
The Canaanite Woman's Daughter
Matthew 15:21-28
A Canaanite woman begged Jesus to heal her daughter. Jesus tested her faith. She persisted: "Even the dogs eat the crumbs from the master's table." Jesus said, "Great is your faith!"
Her persistence was rewarded. What keeps us praying when God seems silent?
Young ones: Did the woman give up or keep asking? What happened?
24
Feeding the Four Thousand
Matthew 15:32-39
Again Jesus fed a multitude — four thousand this time — with seven loaves and a few fish. Seven baskets of fragments remained.
Jesus said, "I have compassion on the multitude." How does compassion move God to act?
Young ones: How many people were fed this time?
25
The Resurrection of Christ
Matthew 28:1-10
On the third day, the tomb was empty. An angel said, "He is not here; for He is risen, as He said." Christ conquered death — the greatest miracle of all.
The Resurrection changes everything. What does it mean for you personally that Christ is risen?
Young ones: What did the angel say? Is Jesus still in the tomb?
26
Turning Water to Wine at Cana
John 2:1-11
Mary said to the servants, "Whatever He says to you, do it." She trusted before she saw. The servants obeyed without understanding. And the miracle came.
Mary's words to the servants are her words to us. What is Jesus telling you to do that you have not yet done?
Young ones: What did Mary tell the servants to do?
27
The Fig Tree Withered
Mark 11:12-14, 20-25
Jesus cursed a fig tree that had leaves but no fruit. By the next morning it had withered. He said, "Have faith in God."
The fig tree had the appearance of life but no fruit. Where do we have appearance without substance?
Young ones: What was wrong with the fig tree? What did Jesus teach about it?

Miracles and Mercy — Page 4 of 4

28
Jesus Heals on the Sabbath
Luke 13:10-17
A woman bent double for eighteen years was healed by Jesus on the Sabbath. She stood straight and glorified God. The synagogue ruler was indignant.
Eighteen years bent over. What burdens do people carry for years that God wants to lift?
Young ones: How long was the woman bent over? What did she do when Jesus healed her?
29
The Stilling of the Storm
Luke 8:22-25
"Where is your faith?" Jesus asked after stilling the storm. The disciples were amazed: "Who can this be? For He commands even the winds and water, and they obey Him!"
In the middle of the storm, where was Jesus? What does this tell us about where He is in our storms?
Young ones: Where was Jesus during the storm? Was He worried?
30
The Ascension
Acts 1:9-11
After forty days with His disciples, Jesus ascended into heaven. Two angels said, "This same Jesus will come again in like manner as you saw Him go."
Jesus ascended but did not leave us. How is He present with us now?
The angels said He will return. How does this hope shape our daily life?
Young ones: Where did Jesus go? Will He come back?
