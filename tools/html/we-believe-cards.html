🤔
What does "begotten, not made" mean?
Hint: The Son is born from the Father's nature, not created like us.
📚
Who was Arius, and what did he teach that was wrong?
Hint: He said the Son was created and not truly God.
🛡
Which saint defended the Creed against the whole world?
Hint: "Athanasius contra mundum" — Athanasius against the world.
🏢
In what city and year was the Creed first proclaimed?
Hint: A city in modern-day Turkey, in the year 325 AD.

We Believe — Coptic Cross Games — Page 5 of 6 (Challenge Cards 7-15)

✝
What does "of one essence with the Father" mean?
Hint: The Greek word is homoousios. The Son shares the same divine nature.
🕊
What does "Pantocrator" mean?
Hint: Look at the icon in the dome of the church. It means "Almighty" or "Ruler of All."
🔥
Why does the Creed say the Holy Spirit "proceeds from the Father" and not "from the Father and the Son"?
Hint: The Orthodox Church follows John 15:26 exactly.
💧
Why does the Creed say "one baptism for the remission of sins"?
Hint: Baptism can only happen once because we are born again once.
🌟
What does it mean that the Church is "catholic"?
Hint: It does not mean Roman Catholic. The Greek word means "universal" — for all people, everywhere.
📖
What does "according to the Scriptures" tell us about the Resurrection?
Hint: The prophets foretold it. It was always God's plan.
🤔
What does "was incarnate of the Holy Spirit and of the Virgin Mary" mean?
Hint: God became truly human while remaining truly God.
🌞
What does "Light of Light" mean?
Hint: Like a candle lit from another candle — the same fire, not a lesser one.
⚡
What does "apostolic" mean when we say the Church is apostolic?
Hint: Our bishops trace their ordination back to the Apostles themselves.
