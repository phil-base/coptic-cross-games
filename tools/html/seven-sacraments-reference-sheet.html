The Seven Sacraments

Quick Reference Sheet — Coptic Cross Games

The seven sacraments (holy mysteries) of the Coptic Orthodox Church, their meaning, and their elements.

Sacrament Meaning Scripture Five Elements When Received
Baptism New birth in Christ; death to sin and resurrection to new life through water and the Spirit. John 3:5; Matthew 28:19 Water of the Jordan, Holy Oil (Ghaliloun), Three Immersions, Renunciation of Satan, White Garment Infancy (40 days for boys, 80 days for girls) or upon conversion.
Chrismation The seal of the Holy Spirit; confirmation of the baptised as God's own. Acts 8:14-17; 1 John 2:20 Holy Myron, 36 Anointings, Seal of the Holy Spirit, Breath of the Priest, Girdle of Faith Immediately after Baptism.
Confession Reconciliation with God; forgiveness of sins committed after Baptism. John 20:22-23; 1 John 1:9 Repentant Heart, Absolution Prayer, The Epitrachelion, Examination of Conscience, Penance and Counsel Regularly throughout life; required before Holy Communion.
Eucharist Receiving the true Body and Blood of Christ for eternal life. John 6:53-56; Luke 22:19-20 The Lamb (Bread), The Wine, The Epiclesis, Body and Blood of Christ, The Fraction Regularly throughout life at the Divine Liturgy.
Anointing of the Sick Healing of body and soul through prayer, oil, and the laying on of hands. James 5:14-15; Mark 6:13 Oil of the Sick, Seven Prayers, Laying on of Hands, Anointing of the Senses, Prayer of Faith During serious illness or as needed.
Matrimony The union of man and woman in Christ, reflecting Christ and His Church. Ephesians 5:31-32; John 2:1-11 The Crowns, Rings of Betrothal, The Blessing Prayer, Unity of Flesh, Psalm of Joy When a man and woman enter holy marriage.
Priesthood Ordination to serve God's people as deacon, priest, or bishop through apostolic succession. Acts 6:6; 1 Timothy 4:14; 2 Timothy 1:6 Laying on of Hands, Breath of the Holy Spirit, The Vestments, Apostolic Succession, The Ordination Prayer When called and ordained by a bishop.
