💧
Water of the Jordan
The baptismal water recalls the Jordan where Christ was baptised, sanctifying all water for our new birth.
Baptism
🧴
Holy Oil (Ghaliloun)
The oil of gladness anoints the catechumen before immersion, preparing the body to receive the Holy Spirit.
Baptism
♻
Three Immersions
Three full immersions in the name of the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit — we die and rise with Christ.
Baptism
🙅
Renunciation of Satan
The catechumen faces west and renounces Satan and all his works, then turns east to confess Christ.
Baptism
👖
White Garment
The newly baptised is clothed in white, symbolising the purity of the new life received in Christ.
Baptism
🎆
Holy Myron
The sacred chrism oil consecrated by the patriarch, carrying a tradition unbroken since the apostles.
Chrismation
✝
36 Anointings
The priest anoints 36 parts of the body with Holy Myron, sanctifying every member for God's service.
Chrismation
🕊
Seal of the Holy Spirit
Each anointing seals the person as God's own, marking them with the gift of the Holy Spirit.
Chrismation
🌬
Breath of the Priest
The priest breathes on the face of the newly anointed, recalling how God breathed life into Adam.
Chrismation

The Seven Sacraments — Coptic Cross Games — Page 2 of 5 (Chrismation cont. + Confession + Eucharist)

🛑
Girdle of Faith
A cord tied around the newly chrismated, symbolising being girded with the armour of faith.
Chrismation
💔
Repentant Heart
True confession begins with a contrite heart that sincerely turns back to God in sorrow and hope.
Confession
📜
Absolution Prayer
The priest prays the absolution, and through the authority given by Christ, sins are forgiven.
Confession
🏬
The Epitrachelion
The priestly stole placed over the penitent's head, signifying the priest's authority to absolve sins.
Confession
🔍
Examination of Conscience
Before confession, we examine our thoughts, words, and deeds honestly before God.
Confession
📚
Penance and Counsel
The father of confession gives guidance and a penance to help the penitent grow in virtue.
Confession
🍞
The Lamb (Bread)
Pure bread chosen and prepared for the liturgy, which will become the Body of our Lord.
Eucharist
🍷
The Wine
Pure grape wine mixed with water, which will become the precious Blood of Christ in the chalice.
Eucharist
🔥
The Epiclesis
The invocation of the Holy Spirit upon the bread and wine to transform them into Christ's Body and Blood.
Eucharist

The Seven Sacraments — Coptic Cross Games — Page 3 of 5 (Eucharist cont. + Anointing of the Sick + Matrimony partial)

✨
Body and Blood of Christ
The bread and wine truly become the Body and Blood of our Lord — the mystery of all mysteries.
Eucharist
✌
The Fraction
The priest breaks the Lamb while praying, recalling Christ's body broken for us on the cross.
Eucharist
🧴
Oil of the Sick
Holy oil blessed by the priest for anointing the sick, following the apostolic practice of James 5:14.
Anointing of the Sick
🙏
Seven Prayers
Seven prayers are read from the Epistles and Gospels, asking God's healing mercy for the sick person.
Anointing of the Sick
🖐
Laying on of Hands
The priest lays hands on the sick person, an apostolic gesture that channels God's healing power.
Anointing of the Sick
💜
Anointing of the Senses
The senses are anointed with oil — forehead, nostrils, mouth, ears, hands — asking healing for the whole person.
Anointing of the Sick
🙌
Prayer of Faith
"The prayer of faith shall save the sick" — the community prays in faith, trusting in God's mercy.
Anointing of the Sick
👑
The Crowns
The bride and groom are crowned as king and queen of their new household, crowned in God's glory.
Matrimony
💍
Rings of Betrothal
The rings are exchanged as a sign of the covenant promise between husband and wife before God.
Matrimony

The Seven Sacraments — Coptic Cross Games — Page 4 of 5 (Matrimony cont. + Priesthood)

😊
The Blessing Prayer
The priest prays for God's blessing on the marriage, asking for unity, peace, and faithfulness.
Matrimony
🤝
Unity of Flesh
The two become one flesh as Christ taught — a mystery reflecting Christ's union with His Church.
Matrimony
🎶
Psalm of Joy
Psalms of joy and praise are chanted as the couple processes, celebrating the grace of holy marriage.
Matrimony
🖐
Laying on of Hands
The bishop lays hands on the ordinand, transmitting the grace of the priesthood through apostolic succession.
Priesthood
🌬
Breath of the Holy Spirit
The bishop breathes on the ordinand, as Christ breathed on the apostles saying "Receive the Holy Spirit."
Priesthood
👕
The Vestments
The new priest is vested in the priestly garments, each piece carrying spiritual significance and responsibility.
Priesthood
🔗
Apostolic Succession
An unbroken chain of ordination from the apostles to today, preserving the fullness of the priesthood.
Priesthood
📜
The Ordination Prayer
The bishop prays the ancient prayer of ordination, calling down the Holy Spirit upon the new priest.
Priesthood
