Archangels and Angels

A matching game for young children — Ages 4-7 — 2-4 Players — 10 Minutes

Age Range
4-7
Players
2-4
Play Time
10 minutes
Difficulty
Easy

About This Game

Archangels and Angels introduces young children to the seven archangels honoured in the Coptic Orthodox Church. By matching each archangel to their role, children learn that God sends His angels to protect, guide, heal, and carry our prayers.

Formation Goal: Knowing that God sends His angels to protect and guide us — and that we are never alone.

Components

  • 7 Archangel Cards — each showing the archangel's name and title
  • 7 Role Cards — each describing what that archangel does, with a hint question
  • 4 Scripture Cards — Bible verses about angels (for older children or bonus matching)

Setup

  1. Print the card sheets on cardstock (110 lb recommended).
  2. Cut along the card borders.
  3. For the simplest game, use only the 7 Archangel Cards and 7 Role Cards (14 cards total).
  4. Shuffle all chosen cards together.
  5. Lay the cards face-down in a grid (good sizes: 4×4 with extras, or 2×7).

How to Play — Memory Mode

  1. The youngest player goes first.
  2. On your turn, flip over any two cards so everyone can see them.
  3. If the cards match (an archangel and their role): keep the pair and take another turn.
  4. If the cards do not match: flip them back face-down, and the next player goes.
  5. When a match is found, read the role card together. Say: "Thank you, [Archangel name], for [what they do]!"
  6. Continue until all pairs are found. The player with the most pairs wins!

How to Play — Sorting Mode

For the very youngest children (ages 3-5):

  1. Lay all cards face-up on the table.
  2. Work together to match each archangel to their role.
  3. Read each pair together and talk about what the archangel does.
  4. End with a prayer asking the archangels to watch over you.

The Seven Archangels

  • Michael — Protects God's people and fights against evil
  • Gabriel — Brings God's messages (told St. Mary about Jesus!)
  • Raphael — Heals the sick and guides travellers
  • Suriel — Carries souls to God when they leave this world
  • Sedakiel — Leads worship and praise before God's throne
  • Sarathiel — Carries our prayers up to God
  • Ananiel — Watches over rain, wind, and all of nature

Tips for Parents and Teachers

  • Start with just 3-4 archangels for the youngest children.
  • Point out the archangels' icons in church — especially Michael, who is in every Coptic church.
  • Play on the 12th of any Coptic month, when Archangel Michael is commemorated.
  • At bedtime, help your child pray: "Archangel Michael, protect me while I sleep."
