Miracles and Mercy

A family discussion game — All Ages — 3-8 Players — 25 Minutes

Age Range
All ages
Players
3-8
Play Time
25 minutes
Difficulty
Easy

About This Game

The miracles of Christ are not just ancient wonders. They reveal who God is — compassionate, powerful, near to the suffering, and faithful to His people. Miracles and Mercy invites the whole family to explore these signs and discover what they mean for our lives today.

Formation Goal: Connecting miracles to faith, prayer, and the mercy of God — not as past spectacle but as present reality through the sacraments and intercession of the saints.

Components

  • 30 Miracle Cards — each with a miracle, Scripture reference, narrative, and discussion questions
  • Rules (this sheet)
  • Leader Guide — deeper theological reflections for facilitators

Setup

  1. Print the miracle cards on cardstock and cut along borders.
  2. Shuffle and place face-down in a stack.
  3. Sit together as a family or group.
  4. Open with the Lord's Prayer or a brief invocation.

How to Play

  1. The youngest player draws a card and hands it to an adult to read aloud.
  2. Read the miracle title, the Scripture reference, and the brief narrative.
  3. Start with the young ones question (in gold text) so the smallest children can participate.
  4. Then discuss the main questions together. There are no right answers — the conversation is the point.
  5. Draw another card. Continue for as many rounds as feel natural (5-8 miracles is typical for one session).

Tips for Families

  • Let the young children answer first. Their simplicity often opens the conversation beautifully.
  • It is fine to say "I don't know." Miracles are mysterious by nature.
  • Connect to real life: "Has God ever done something surprising for our family?"
  • Connect to church: "Remember when we heard this Gospel reading in the liturgy?"
  • Do not rush. Three miracles discussed well are better than ten read quickly.

Variants

  • Bedtime version: One miracle card per night as part of bedtime routine.
  • Liturgical pairing: Use the miracle card that matches this week's Gospel reading.
  • Art response: After discussing, invite children to draw the miracle.
  • Acting out: Young children can act out the miracle after hearing it.
