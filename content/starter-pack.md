## Start Here

The best way to understand formation through play is to experience it. Download our free starter games and try them with your family, Sunday school class, or youth group.

## Free Starter Games

### For Young Children: Saints Memory

**[Saints Memory](/games/saints-memory/)** is a simple matching game for ages 4-7. Children flip cards to find pairs of Coptic saints matched to their symbols — St. George and the dragon, St. Mary and the Christ child, St. Mina and the two camels.

- 24 printable cards (12 saint pairs)
- 1-page rules sheet
- Plays in 15 minutes

### For Children: Salvation History Timeline

**[Salvation History Timeline](/games/salvation-history-timeline/)** challenges players to place biblical events in order on a shared timeline, building a picture of God's plan from creation to Pentecost.

- 60 event cards with Scripture references
- Printable timeline board
- 2-page rules and 4-page leader guide
- Plays in 25 minutes

### For Teens: The Narrow Way

**[The Narrow Way](/games/the-narrow-way/)** is a push-your-luck game exploring the tension between comfort and faithfulness. Persevere in virtue or retreat to safety — but only the narrow way leads to life.

- 40 cards (situations and trials)
- Score track
- 2-page rules and 6-page leader guide
- Plays in 25 minutes

### For Adults: Desert Fathers Wisdom

**[Desert Fathers Wisdom](/games/desert-fathers-wisdom/)** brings the sayings of the desert fathers and mothers to the table for honest, shared reflection. No points. No winner. The conversation is the game.

- 40 saying cards
- Facilitation guide and 8-page leader guide
- Reflection sheet for journaling
- Plays in 30 minutes

### For Families: Feast Day Match

**[Feast Day Match](/games/feast-day-match/)** is a colorful matching game pairing Coptic feasts with their icons and stories. The whole family can play together.

- 24 printable cards (12 feast pairs)
- 1-page rules
- Plays in 15 minutes

## How to Print

1. Download the PDF files from each game page
2. Print cards on cardstock (110 lb recommended) for durability
3. Cut along the guides
4. Print rules and leader guides on regular paper

That's it. You're ready to play.

## What to Expect

Most games take 15-30 minutes to play. The discussion afterward — guided by the Leader Guide — may take another 10-15 minutes. That conversation is where the real formation happens.
