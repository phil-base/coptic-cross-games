## Overview

"Come, follow Me." (Matthew 4:19)

The Gospels are not biographies — they are invitations. The Life of Christ is a cooperative journey game where players walk together through the major events of Jesus' earthly ministry, from the Annunciation to the Ascension. At each stop, the group faces a challenge that mirrors what the disciples experienced: will you trust when it's hard? Will you serve when it costs? Will you stay when others leave?

## How It Works

The board traces a path through the life of Christ, divided into five stages: **Birth and Childhood**, **Baptism and Temptation**, **Ministry and Teaching**, **Passion and Cross**, and **Resurrection and Ascension**.

Players move together as a group. At each stop, an **Event Card** is drawn describing a Gospel event. Some events are blessings (the Transfiguration, the feeding of the five thousand) that grant the group resources. Others are trials (the storm at sea, Gethsemane, the denial of Peter) that require players to spend Faith, Courage, or Love tokens to overcome.

Players share tokens freely. If the group reaches the Ascension together with at least one token remaining, everyone wins. The journey is the point — and what you learn along the way matters more than the finish.

## What Makes It Formation

The Coptic lectionary walks the faithful through the life of Christ every year. This game mirrors that journey in miniature. The cooperative mechanic teaches that following Christ is not a solitary endeavour — the disciples walked together, fell together, and were restored together. The resource-sharing models the Church's teaching on bearing one another's burdens.

The five stages align with the liturgical year: Nativity, Epiphany, the Sundays of the year, Holy Week, and the Pentecostal season.

## Components

- **Event Cards** (48) — Gospel events across five stages of Christ's life
- **Board** (1 page) — Journey path from Annunciation to Ascension
- **Rules** (2 pages) — Cooperative gameplay and token management
- **Leader Guide** (6 pages) — Gospel reflections for each event, discussion prompts, and liturgical connections

## The Five Stages

1. **Birth and Childhood** — Annunciation, Nativity, Flight to Egypt, Presentation in the Temple
2. **Baptism and Temptation** — John the Baptist, the Jordan, forty days in the wilderness
3. **Ministry and Teaching** — Calling the disciples, miracles, parables, the Transfiguration
4. **Passion and Cross** — Palm Sunday, the Last Supper, Gethsemane, the Crucifixion, burial
5. **Resurrection and Ascension** — The empty tomb, appearances, the Great Commission, the Ascension
