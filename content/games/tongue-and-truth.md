## Overview

"Death and life are in the power of the tongue." (Proverbs 18:21)

Tongue and Truth presents teens with realistic scenarios involving speech — gossip at school, lies to avoid trouble, harsh words in anger, flattery to gain favor, silence when a friend is being mistreated — and invites them to practice discernment in how they use their words.

## How It Works

A **Scenario Card** is read aloud: a real-life situation involving speech. Each player selects a **Response Card** that best represents what they would do. Players reveal simultaneously, then discuss.

Response cards range from clearly wrong ("Spread the gossip to everyone") to clearly right ("Speak the truth with kindness") to genuinely ambiguous ("Stay silent to avoid conflict"). The ambiguous ones are where the real formation happens.

## What Makes It Formation

The desert fathers considered control of the tongue one of the hardest and most important ascetic practices. St. James writes that "the tongue is a fire, a world of iniquity" (James 3:6). Yet the same tongue that destroys can also heal, encourage, and speak truth.

This game teaches teens to pause before speaking — and to consider the spiritual weight of every word.
