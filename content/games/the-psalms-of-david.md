## Overview

The Psalms are the prayer book of the Church. For two thousand years, the Coptic Church has structured her daily prayer around the Psalter — in the Agpeya, in the liturgy, and in the midnight praises. The Psalms of David helps players discover why specific Psalms are prayed at specific hours and how the Psalter speaks to every human condition.

## How It Works

Players receive cards featuring Psalm verses, each marked with its mood (praise, penitence, trust, lament, thanksgiving) and its traditional hour of prayer. Players arrange their cards into coherent prayer sequences — building a morning prayer, an evening prayer, or a prayer for a specific need.

Correct patterns (matching the Church's own arrangement in the Agpeya) score highest, but creative arrangements that demonstrate understanding of the Psalms' spiritual logic also score.

## What Makes It Formation

Most Christians know a handful of Psalms (23, 51, 91) but have never experienced the Psalter as a unified prayer book. This game opens the door to the Church's ancient practice of praying the hours — and reveals that the Psalms contain prayers for every situation a human soul can face.
