## Overview

In the Coptic Church, those who serve — Sunday school teachers, deacons, choir members, hospitality workers — are called "servants" (khaddam). The Faithful Servant invites children to experience what it means to serve in the church: not as performers seeking praise, but as members of the Body of Christ offering their gifts.

## How It Works

Players take roles as different church servants: the Sunday school teacher, the deacon, the choir member, the hospitality worker, the visitor of the sick. Each role has unique abilities.

Together, the group faces a series of **Service Challenges**: preparing for a feast day, welcoming a new family, supporting a grieving member, organizing a youth retreat. Players must allocate shared resources — Prayer, Preparation, Time, and Love — to meet each challenge.

The key: every service task begins with prayer. Players who skip prayer to "save time" find their efforts less effective.

## What Makes It Formation

The game teaches that service without prayer is empty, that all servants work together as one body, and that the goal of service is not recognition but faithfulness. These are core Coptic values about khedma (service).
