## Overview

"He shall give His angels charge over you, to keep you in all your ways." (Psalm 91:11)

Young children love angels — and the Coptic Church has a rich tradition of angelic devotion. Archangels and Angels is a simple matching game that introduces the youngest players to the heavenly hosts: Michael the protector, Gabriel the messenger, Raphael the healer, and the others who serve God and watch over His people.

## How It Works

Cards show either an **Archangel Card** (with their name and a simple illustration) or a **Role Card** (describing what that archangel does). Children match each archangel to their role.

Play as a **memory game** (cards face-down, flip two at a time to find matches) or as a **sorting game** (cards face-up, group the archangels with their roles). Both modes are designed for very young children with simple, clear illustrations and short text.

After each match, the parent or teacher reads the short prayer or verse on the back of the Archangel Card. Children learn not just *who* the angels are but *how* the Church asks for their help.

## What Makes It Formation

Angels are not fairy-tale creatures — they are real servants of God who stand before His throne and are sent to protect His people. The Coptic Church commemorates the archangels monthly and prays to them daily in the Agpeya. This game plants the seeds of that awareness: that the child is surrounded by God's messengers, that the unseen world is real, and that the Church has always known their names.

## The Archangels

1. **Michael** — The protector, leader of the heavenly armies, who fights for God's people
2. **Gabriel** — The messenger, who brought the good news to the Virgin Mary
3. **Raphael** — The healer, who accompanied Tobias and healed Tobit's blindness
4. **Suriel** — The trumpeter, angel of death who carries souls to God
5. **Sedakiel** — The angel of worship and praise
6. **Sarathiel** — The angel of prayer, who carries prayers before God's throne
7. **Ananiel** — The angel of rain, winds, and the natural world
