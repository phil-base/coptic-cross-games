## Overview

"What man of you, having a hundred sheep, if he loses one of them, does not leave the ninety-nine in the wilderness, and go after the one which is lost until he finds it?" (Luke 15:4)

The Good Shepherd is a cooperative game for the youngest players. The shepherd has lost his sheep in the hills, the valley, and the forest. Night is coming. Players work together to find every lost sheep and bring them home before the sun sets. It is a game about the love of Christ — a love that counts every one, notices every absence, and never stops searching.

## How It Works

The board shows a simple landscape with five locations: the **Hills**, the **Valley**, the **Forest**, the **River**, and the **Sheepfold** (home). Ten sheep tokens are hidden face-down across the locations.

Players take turns drawing a **Path Card** that lets the shepherd move to a location and reveal a sheep. Some cards show **Obstacles** (a storm, a thorny bush, a steep cliff) that require two players to work together to overcome. Some show **Blessings** (a clear path, a still stream, a guiding star) that let the shepherd rescue two sheep at once.

A simple day/night tracker counts down. If all sheep are home before nightfall, everyone wins and celebrates together — just as the shepherd's neighbours rejoiced with him.

## What Makes It Formation

The parable of the lost sheep is one of the first stories Coptic children hear. This game makes it physical and personal: the children *are* the shepherd, and they feel the urgency of searching, the frustration of obstacles, and the joy of finding. The cooperative mechanic means no child is left out — just as no sheep is left behind. The game's message is the Gospel in miniature: you are loved, you are sought, and there is joy in heaven over you.
