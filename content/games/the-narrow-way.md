## Overview

"Enter by the narrow gate; for wide is the gate and broad is the way that leads to destruction, and there are many who go in by it. Because narrow is the gate and difficult is the way which leads to life, and there are few who find it." (Matthew 7:13-14)

The Narrow Way makes this teaching visceral. Each turn, players choose between safety and faithfulness — and discover that the narrow way, though harder, produces something the broad way never can.

## How It Works

Each round presents a **Situation Card** with two paths: the **Broad Way** (safe, comfortable, immediately rewarding) and the **Narrow Way** (costly, difficult, but bearing lasting fruit).

Choosing the Narrow Way means drawing from the **Trial Deck**. Most trials are passable — requiring prayer, patience, or sacrifice. But some are severe. If you pass, you gain Virtue Points and advance. If you fail, you lose your progress for that round.

The tension between pressing on and playing it safe creates the game's central experience.

## What Makes It Formation

The game does not moralize. It lets players feel the real cost of virtue and the real emptiness of comfort. The discussion that follows — guided by the Leader Guide — is where formation deepens.

The ascetic tradition of the Coptic Church teaches that the narrow way is not punishment but liberation. Fasting frees us from appetite. Prayer frees us from distraction. Self-denial frees us from slavery to comfort.

## Components

- **Situation Cards** (20) — Real-life scenarios with two paths
- **Trial Cards** (20) — Challenges on the narrow way
- **Score Track** (1 page) — Virtue point progression
- **Rules** (2 pages) — Push-your-luck mechanics
- **Leader Guide** (6 pages) — Patristic reflections on each situation
