## Overview

The truths we confess every Sunday in the Creed were not handed to us easily. They were defended by bishops who suffered exile, imprisonment, and persecution for the sake of the true faith. Defenders of the Faith tells their story through a set-collection game that teaches the councils and the heroes who stood firm.

## How It Works

Each complete set consists of four cards: a **Council** (where and when), a **Defender** (who stood for truth), a **Heresy** (what was opposed), and a **Truth** (what was preserved). Players draw and trade cards to complete sets.

When a set is completed, the player reads aloud: "At the Council of [X], [Saint] defended the truth that [Y] against the teaching that [Z]."

## What Makes It Formation

The Coptic Church played a central role in the early councils. St. Athanasius was exiled five times for defending Nicaea. Pope Dioscorus stood firm at Chalcedon. These are not abstract historical events — they are the defining moments of Coptic identity.

Learning the councils teaches teens why we believe what we believe and what it cost to preserve it.

## Components

- **Cards** (60) — Council, Defender, Heresy, and Truth cards
- **Reference Sheet** (1 page) — Summary of all councils and their outcomes
- **Rules** (2 pages) — Set-collection mechanics
- **Leader Guide** (6 pages) — Extended council histories and discussion prompts

## Councils Covered

- Nicaea (325) — St. Athanasius, Arianism, the divinity of Christ
- Constantinople (381) — The Cappadocian Fathers, the Holy Spirit
- Ephesus (431) — St. Cyril of Alexandria, Nestorianism, the Theotokos
- And their theological legacy in Coptic doctrine
