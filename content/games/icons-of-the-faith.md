## Overview

"We do not worship icons — we venerate the persons they depict, and through them we are drawn upward to God."

Coptic icons are not merely art. They are theology written in colour and form. Every gesture, every colour, every arrangement carries meaning rooted in centuries of tradition. Icons of the Faith teaches players to *read* icons — to recognise the saints, understand the symbols, and enter into the theology that icons proclaim.

## How It Works

The game uses two card types: **Icon Cards** showing details from Coptic icons (a hand gesture, a colour palette, an object held by a saint, a background element) and **Meaning Cards** explaining the theological significance of each detail.

In the main game mode, Icon Cards are laid face-up in a grid. Players take turns drawing a Meaning Card and claiming the matching Icon Card. If challenged by another player, the group consults the reference sheet. Correct matches score; incorrect matches return both cards.

In the **Icon Detective** variant, one player describes an icon detail from a Meaning Card without naming it, and others race to identify the correct Icon Card — building visual literacy through discussion.

## What Makes It Formation

The Coptic tradition holds that icons make the invisible visible. They teach theology to those who cannot read, comfort the suffering with the presence of the saints, and draw the worshipper's gaze upward. By learning to read icons, players learn to see the church building itself as a catechism — every wall, every image, every colour proclaiming the faith.

## Components

- **Icon Cards** (36) — Details and elements from Coptic icons

- **Rules** (2 pages) — Main game and Icon Detective variant
- **Leader Guide** (6 pages) — History of Coptic iconography, teaching on veneration, and discussion prompts

## Iconographic Elements Include

- **Colours**: Gold (divinity), blue (heaven), red (martyrdom/divinity), white (purity/resurrection)
- **Gestures**: Blessing hand, open palms (prayer), hand over heart (intercession)
- **Objects**: Cross, scroll, censer, Gospel book, palm branch (martyrdom), crown
- **Conventions**: Large eyes (spiritual sight), small mouth (silence/prayer), frontal gaze (presence with the viewer)
- **Saints**: St. Mary, St. Mark, St. George, St. Mina, the Archangel Michael
