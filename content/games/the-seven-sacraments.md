## Overview

"Unless one is born of water and the Spirit, he cannot enter the kingdom of God." (John 3:5)

The sacraments are not ceremonies the Church invented — they are the means by which Christ continues to give His life to His people. In The Seven Sacraments, players collect the elements that make up each mystery: the water and oil of Baptism, the myron of Chrismation, the bread and wine of the Eucharist, the prayers of Confession, and more. Along the way, they learn what each sacrament is, why the Church celebrates it, and how it connects to the life of Christ.

## How It Works

The deck contains **Element Cards** in seven suits, one for each sacrament: Baptism, Chrismation, Confession, Eucharist, Anointing of the Sick, Matrimony, and Priesthood. Each suit has 4-5 cards representing the visible elements and actions of that sacrament (water, oil, laying on of hands, prayer, etc.).

Players draw from a shared deck and trade cards with one another, trying to complete full sacrament sets. When a player completes a set, they lay it down and read the **Sacrament Summary** from the reference sheet — a short explanation of what that mystery gives us.

The game ends when all seven sacraments have been claimed. The player who completed the most sets wins, but every completed sacrament is celebrated by the group.

## What Makes It Formation

The Coptic Church teaches that the sacraments are not symbolic gestures but real encounters with God. By handling the elements — learning that Baptism requires water *and* oil *and* the invocation of the Holy Spirit — children begin to understand that these mysteries are rich, layered, and intentional. The trading mechanic reinforces that the sacramental life is communal: we receive these gifts within the Body of Christ, not alone.

## Components

- **Element Cards** (35) — 5 cards per sacrament, each showing an element or action
- **Reference Sheet** (1 page) — Sacrament summaries and Scripture connections
- **Rules** (2 pages) — Setup, trading, and set completion
- **Leader Guide** (4 pages) — Teaching notes on each sacrament with patristic references

## The Seven Mysteries

1. **Baptism** — Water, oil, immersion, renunciation, new birth
2. **Chrismation** — Holy Myron, 36 anointings, the seal of the Holy Spirit
3. **Confession** — Repentance, absolution prayer, the priest's epitrachelion
4. **Eucharist** — Bread, wine, the Epiclesis, the Body and Blood of Christ
5. **Anointing of the Sick** — Oil, seven prayers, healing of body and soul
6. **Matrimony** — Crowns, prayers, the union blessed by Christ
7. **Priesthood** — Laying on of hands, the breath of the Spirit, apostolic succession
