## Overview

The Coptic Church does not experience time the way the world does. Every season has its spirit, every month its saints, every week its rhythm of fasting and feasting. Seasons of Grace teaches children to feel this rhythm by building the liturgical year piece by piece.

## How It Works

Players receive cards representing feasts, fasts, and liturgical seasons. A circular calendar board sits in the center. Players take turns placing cards in their correct positions on the calendar — Nayrouz (Coptic New Year), the Nativity Fast, Christmas, Epiphany, Jonah's Fast, Great Lent, Holy Week, the Glorious Feast of the Resurrection, Pentecost, the Apostles' Fast, and more.

Correct placements score. The calendar fills up as the game progresses, and the whole year becomes visible.

## What Makes It Formation

When children can see the whole liturgical year laid out, they understand that the Church's life has a shape. Fasting prepares for feasting. Lent leads to Pascha. The rhythm of the year forms the soul — not through lectures, but through lived experience.

## Components

- **Season Cards** (40) — Feasts, fasts, and seasons with dates and descriptions

- **Rules** (2 pages) — Placement and scoring
- **Leader Guide** (4 pages) — Explanations of each season and feast
