## Overview

"We believe in one God, the Father Almighty, Maker of heaven and earth..."

Every Sunday, the Coptic Church stands and confesses the Nicene Creed — the same words defended by St. Athanasius against the whole world, affirmed at the Council of Nicaea in 325 AD, and completed at Constantinople in 381 AD. We Believe turns the Creed into a hands-on learning experience where players race to arrange the phrases in correct order, discovering that every word matters and every clause tells us something essential about God.

## How It Works

The Creed is divided into **Phrase Cards** — short, natural segments of the text. Each card contains one phrase ("Light of Light", "True God of True God", "Who for us men and for our salvation") with a colour band indicating which article of the Creed it belongs to: the Father, the Son, the Holy Spirit, the Church, or the final declarations.

In the **Race Mode**, players each receive a shuffled set of Phrase Cards and race to arrange them in correct order. First to complete the Creed wins. In the **Teaching Mode**, the group works together, placing one phrase at a time and pausing to discuss what each clause means. The leader guide provides a one-paragraph explanation for every phrase.

A **Challenge Deck** adds difficulty: cards ask players to explain a phrase ("What does 'begotten not made' mean?"), connect it to a heresy it refutes ("Why did Arius disagree with this?"), or name the saint who defended it.

## What Makes It Formation

The Creed is not a checklist of beliefs — it is the Church's battle cry. Every phrase was forged in controversy, defended by saints, and sealed with the blood of martyrs. By physically handling each phrase, placing it in order, and discussing its meaning, children begin to understand that the words they say every Sunday carry the weight of the whole tradition behind them.

## Components

- **Phrase Cards** (30) — The complete Nicene Creed in natural phrase segments, colour-coded by article
- **Challenge Cards** (20) — Questions about meaning, history, and the saints who defended the faith
- **Reference Sheet** (1 page) — The full Creed with annotations and historical notes
- **Rules** (2 pages) — Race Mode and Teaching Mode
- **Leader Guide** (6 pages) — Phrase-by-phrase commentary, connections to St. Athanasius and the councils

## The Five Articles

1. **God the Father** — Creator of heaven and earth, seen and unseen
2. **God the Son** — Begotten not made, incarnation, crucifixion, resurrection, ascension, second coming
3. **God the Holy Spirit** — Lord and Giver of Life, proceeding from the Father
4. **The Church** — One, Holy, Catholic, and Apostolic; one baptism; resurrection of the dead
5. **Final Declarations** — The life of the age to come. Amen.
