## Overview

The Church year is full of joy. Christmas, Epiphany, Palm Sunday, the Resurrection, Pentecost — each feast celebrates a moment in the life of Christ that the Church relives every year. Feast Day Match introduces the youngest children to these celebrations through a simple, colorful matching game.

## How It Works

Lay all cards face-down. Flip two at a time. Match a feast name to its picture. Keep matched pairs. The child with the most pairs wins.

Each pair shows the feast name on one card and a simple illustration of the feast on the other — the Nativity star, the baptism in the Jordan, the palm branches, the empty tomb, the tongues of fire.

## What Makes It Formation

Young children experience feasts through sights, sounds, and participation long before they understand theology. This game builds visual recognition that connects to their lived experience: "I remember this one! We had palms at church!"

That connection between game and experience is the beginning of liturgical formation.
