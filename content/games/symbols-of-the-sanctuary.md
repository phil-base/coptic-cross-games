## Overview

Walk into a Coptic church and everything speaks: the icons, the iconostasis, the altar behind the curtain, the censer rising with incense, the cross on every surface. Nothing is decoration. Everything is theology. Symbols of the Sanctuary teaches children to see and understand what surrounds them every Sunday.

## How It Works

Cards come in pairs: an **Object Card** (showing the item with its Coptic and English name) and a **Meaning Card** (explaining what it represents). Players match objects to their spiritual meanings.

Play as a memory game (face-down matching) for younger children, or as a speed-match (face-up, race to find pairs) for older children.

## What Makes It Formation

Children who understand the sanctuary engage more deeply in worship. When they know that the curtain represents the separation between heaven and earth — opened when Christ makes heaven accessible — the liturgy becomes alive to them.
