## Overview

The desert fathers and mothers are the spiritual ancestors of the Coptic Church. Their sayings — brief, often paradoxical, always penetrating — have guided souls toward God for seventeen centuries. Desert Fathers Wisdom brings these sayings to the table for honest, shared reflection.

## How It Works

Draw a card. Read the saying aloud. Sit with it for a moment of silence. Then each player shares their response: What strikes you? What challenges you? What do you not understand?

There are no points. There is no winner. The game is the conversation — and the silence between the words.

The **Leader Guide** provides context for each saying: who said it, what was happening in the desert at the time, and how the saying connects to our lives now.

## What Makes It Formation

The desert tradition is the foundation of Coptic spirituality. St. Anthony, St. Macarius, St. Pachomius, St. Moses the Black, Amma Syncletica — these are not museum figures but living guides. Their words cut through the noise of modern life with startling clarity.

This game does not trivialize their wisdom. It presents it simply and lets it do its work.

## Sayings Include

- "I saw all the snares of the enemy spread over the earth, and I said groaning, 'Who can escape such snares?' And I heard a voice saying to me, 'Humility.'" — St. Anthony
- "If you see a young man climbing to heaven by his own will, grab his foot and pull him down, for it is not profitable for him." — St. Macarius
- "Go, sit in your cell, and your cell will teach you everything." — St. Moses the Black
