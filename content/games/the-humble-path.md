## Overview

St. Anthony said, "I saw all the snares of the enemy spread over the earth, and I said groaning, 'Who can escape such snares?' And I heard a voice saying to me, 'Humility.'"

Humility is the foundation of all virtues in the Coptic tradition — and pride is the subtlest of all vices. The Humble Path teaches players to recognize the difference through a light social deduction game rooted in the desert tradition.

## How It Works

Each player secretly receives a **Role Card**: Humble or Proud. Over several rounds, the group discusses scenarios drawn from church life, family life, and personal spirituality.

Humble players answer honestly and with self-awareness. Proud players subtly inject pride into their responses — not obviously, but in the ways pride actually operates: false modesty, spiritual comparison, subtle self-promotion, judgmentalism disguised as concern.

At the end, the group votes on who they believe carries pride. Then roles are revealed. Discussion follows: How did pride disguise itself? What made humility recognizable? Where do we see these patterns in ourselves?

## What Makes It Formation

Pride is hard to detect precisely because it disguises itself — even as humility. The desert fathers warned about this constantly. This game creates a safe space to practice discernment of this foundational dynamic — and to turn the mirror on oneself.
