## Overview

"But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, longsuffering, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, self-control." (Galatians 5:22-23)

Fruits of the Spirit introduces young children to these nine gifts through a colorful, simple card game. Each fruit is illustrated and connected to a simple action a child can understand.

## How It Works

Shuffle the fruit cards and deal them face-down. Players take turns drawing from the pile or trading with each other. The goal: collect one card for each of the nine fruits of the Spirit.

When you collect a fruit, name it and describe a time you saw it in someone's life — or a time you want to grow in it. Simple prompts on each card help: "Love is when..." "Patience is when..."

The first player to collect all nine fruits wins — but the real goal is learning to recognize these qualities in everyday life.

## What Makes It Formation

These are not personality traits to develop through willpower. They are fruits of the Holy Spirit — gifts that grow through prayer, sacramental life, and cooperation with God's grace. The game frames them this way even for the youngest players.
