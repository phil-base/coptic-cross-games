## Overview

The Old Testament is not merely history. It is shadow and type — a foreshadowing of the realities fulfilled in Christ and His Church. Types and Shadows invites players to explore these connections with the depth and richness that the Church Fathers brought to their reading of Scripture.

## How It Works

Cards are laid out face-up in a shared display. Each card represents either an Old Testament **type** (the Passover lamb, the manna, the bronze serpent, the Ark of the Covenant) or a New Testament **reality** (Christ's sacrifice, the Eucharist, the Cross, the Theotokos).

Players draft cards one at a time, building personal collections of type-reality connections. Strategic choices matter: the connections you build and the ones you deny to others determine the final score.

## What Makes It Formation

Typology is the Church's own hermeneutic. When St. Paul says the Israelites "drank from the spiritual Rock that followed them, and the Rock was Christ" (1 Corinthians 10:4), he is reading typologically. When the Coptic liturgy pairs Old Testament readings with Gospel passages, it is reading typologically.

This game teaches adults to read Scripture the way the Church has always read it — and to see the sacraments prefigured in the oldest pages of the Bible.
