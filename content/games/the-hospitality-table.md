## Overview

"Do not forget to entertain strangers, for by so doing some have unwittingly entertained angels." (Hebrews 13:2)

Abraham welcomed three visitors at Mamre and received the promise of God. The Hospitality Table invites the whole family to practice the ancient virtue of welcoming others — and to discover that true hospitality is a form of worship.

## How It Works

A guest is coming! The family must prepare: clean, cook, set the table, and — most importantly — prepare their hearts through prayer. Players cooperate to complete **Preparation Cards** using shared resources of Time, Energy, Love, and Prayer.

**Complication Cards** add pressure: unexpected guests, burnt food, a family argument, exhaustion. Can the family maintain joy and warmth under stress, or does hospitality collapse into performance?

The family succeeds if the guest arrives to find a home full of genuine love — not a perfect house.

## What Makes It Formation

Coptic culture places extraordinary value on hospitality — but the spiritual foundation can be lost when hospitality becomes performance or social obligation. This game reconnects the practice to its theological roots: we welcome others because Christ dwells in the stranger.
