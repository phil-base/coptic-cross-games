## Overview

The Coptic spiritual tradition teaches the "royal way" — a balanced path that avoids both laxity and harmful extremism. Walking in Wisdom invites players to draft a personal rule of life from the Church's spiritual practices, discovering that balance, consistency, and the guidance of a confession father are more important than intensity.

## How It Works

**Practice Cards** represent spiritual disciplines: daily Agpeya prayer, fasting, Scripture reading, confession, communion, almsgiving, service, silence, and more. Each card has a "depth level" (beginning, regular, or deep) and a "cost" in time and energy.

Cards are laid out in a shared display. Players draft one card at a time, building a personal rule of life on their **Personal Board**. But there is a constraint: your total "cost" cannot exceed your "capacity" — and **Life Cards** (work, family, health challenges, temptations to overcommit) test whether your rule is sustainable.

The highest-scoring rules are not the most intense — they are the most balanced.

## What Makes It Formation

Many devout people, especially young adults, swing between spiritual overcommitment (leading to burnout) and spiritual collapse (leading to guilt). This game teaches the wisdom of the royal way: steady, sustainable, guided by the Church and a confession father. It is a practical formation tool, not just a game.

## Components

- **Practice Cards** (50) — Spiritual disciplines at three depth levels
- **Life Cards** (20) — Real-life constraints and challenges
- **Personal Board** (1 page) — Rule of life layout
- **Rules** (2 pages) — Drafting and balancing mechanics
- **Leader Guide** (6 pages) — Teaching on the royal way and spiritual balance
- **Reflection Sheet** (1 page) — Draft your actual rule of life after play
