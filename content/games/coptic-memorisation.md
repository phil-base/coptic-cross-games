## Overview

The Coptic language is the living voice of the ancient Egyptian Church — spoken in our hymns, our prayers, and our liturgy. This game helps children and families learn Coptic vocabulary through a hands-on matching mechanic that rewards memory and repetition.

## How It Works

The game uses three decks of cards:

- **The Word Deck** — Coptic words on one side, blank on the other
- **The Picture Deck** — English text and pictures on one side, blank on the other
- **The Double Deck** — Coptic word on one side, the matching picture and English text on the other

In the standard game, the Word Deck is placed face-down in a grid and the Double Deck is placed picture-side-up in a matching grid. Players take turns flipping a word card and then trying to find its match in the Double Deck. If they know the Coptic word, the match is easy. If not, they guess — and learn from each attempt.

## What Makes It Formation

The Coptic language connects us to the earliest Christians in Egypt. When children learn Coptic words — even simple ones like "God," "love," "cross," and "peace" — they are learning the language their spiritual ancestors used to worship. Every word learned is a small act of preservation and devotion.

The game's three variants (matching, pronunciation, and vocabulary) ensure that children encounter Coptic words through multiple pathways: visual recognition, spoken pronunciation, and English-to-Coptic recall.

## Components

- **Word Cards** (15) — Coptic vocabulary words
- **Picture Cards** (15) — English words with illustrations
- **Double Cards** (15) — Coptic on one side, English picture on the other
- **Rules** (1 page) — Instructions for all game variants

## Game Variants

- **Standard Match** — Match Coptic words to pictures using the Word and Double decks
- **Picture Match** — Reverse: match English pictures to hidden Coptic words
- **Pronunciation** — Draw word cards from a bag; keep the card if you can pronounce it correctly
- **Vocabulary** — Draw picture cards from a bag; keep the card if you can say the Coptic word
