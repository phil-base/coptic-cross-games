## Overview

The Agpeya — the Coptic Book of Hours — structures the day around prayer, connecting each hour to an event in Christ's life. The first hour recalls the Resurrection. The third hour recalls Pentecost. The sixth hour recalls the Crucifixion. The Agpeya Hours teaches the whole family this ancient prayer rhythm.

## How It Works

A reference board shows the seven hours of prayer arranged around a clock face. Players receive cards representing Psalms, Gospel readings, themes, and events from Christ's life. Each turn, players match a card to the correct hour.

As the board fills in, the family sees the whole shape of the Agpeya — and begins to understand why the Church prays at specific times.

## What Makes It Formation

The Agpeya is not an optional devotion — it is the prayer backbone of Coptic spiritual life. Priests, monks, and devout laity pray the hours daily. This game opens the door for families to understand and begin practicing this tradition together.

## The Seven Hours

1. **Prime (First Hour)** — Morning, the Resurrection
2. **Terce (Third Hour)** — Mid-morning, Pentecost and the descent of the Holy Spirit
3. **Sext (Sixth Hour)** — Noon, the Crucifixion
4. **None (Ninth Hour)** — Afternoon, Christ's death on the Cross
5. **Vespers (Eleventh Hour)** — Evening, the descent from the Cross
6. **Compline (Twelfth Hour)** — Night, the burial of Christ
7. **Midnight (Veil of Night)** — The Second Coming, watchfulness
