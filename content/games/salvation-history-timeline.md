## Overview

Salvation History Timeline helps players see the Bible not as a collection of isolated stories but as one unfolding narrative of God's faithfulness. From creation through the patriarchs, the Exodus, the prophets, the Incarnation, and Pentecost — every event has its place in God's plan of salvation.

## How It Works

A timeline board is placed in the center with anchor events already positioned: Creation, the Flood, the Exodus, David's Kingdom, the Incarnation, the Crucifixion, and Pentecost.

Each player receives a hand of **Event Cards**. On your turn, place one card where you believe it belongs on the timeline. Other players may challenge your placement.

Correct placements score points. Incorrect placements are corrected and remain on the timeline — so every mistake teaches.

## What Makes It Formation

Most Bible knowledge games test recall of isolated facts. This game builds something deeper: a narrative framework for understanding Scripture as the Coptic Church reads it — as one continuous story in which every event points to Christ.

When players see that Abraham's sacrifice of Isaac prefigures the Cross, or that the Exodus prefigures baptism, they begin to read Scripture the way the Church Fathers read it.

The **Leader Guide** includes brief narrative summaries for each era, connecting gameplay to the larger story the Church tells in her lectionary and liturgy.

## Components

- **Event Cards** (62) — Biblical events with dates, descriptions, and Scripture references
- **Timeline Board** (1 page) — Printable timeline with era markers
- **Rules** (2 pages) — Setup and gameplay
- **Leader Guide** (4 pages) — Era summaries, discussion prompts, and connections to the Coptic lectionary

## Eras Covered

1. **Creation and Patriarchs** — Adam to Joseph in Egypt
2. **Exodus and Conquest** — Moses to Joshua
3. **Judges and Kings** — Deborah to Solomon
4. **Prophets and Exile** — Elijah to the return from Babylon
5. **The Incarnation** — Annunciation to the Ascension
6. **The Early Church** — Pentecost to the Apostolic missions
