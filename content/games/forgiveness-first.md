## Overview

The Coptic liturgy cannot begin without reconciliation. Before the offering of incense, the deacon calls: "Greet one another with a holy kiss. Be reconciled to one another." Forgiveness is not optional in the life of the Church — it is prerequisite.

Forgiveness First invites families and groups to practice this essential Christian discipline through scenarios drawn from everyday life.

## How It Works

A **Scenario Card** describes a situation of hurt or conflict: a sibling breaks a promise, a friend spreads a rumor, a parent is unfair, a church member says something hurtful.

The group discusses together: What happened? How does each person feel? What does forgiveness look like here? What does it not look like? Is reconciliation the same as forgiveness?

The **Leader Guide** provides Scripture references, patristic wisdom, and age-appropriate discussion guidance for each scenario.

There is no winner. The game succeeds when the conversation is honest.

## What Makes It Formation

Forgiveness is simultaneously a commandment ("Forgive us our trespasses as we forgive those who trespass against us"), a grace (we cannot forgive by our own strength), and a process (it may take time). This game holds all three truths together.

The Coptic emphasis on reconciliation before communion, on forgiving before confessing, and on the Lord's Prayer as daily practice makes forgiveness central to spiritual life.

## Components

- **Scenario Cards** (30) — Conflict and hurt situations at different ages and contexts
- **Rules** (1 page) — Facilitation guide
- **Leader Guide** (6 pages) — Scripture, patristic teaching, and discussion prompts
- **Reflection Sheet** (1 page) — Personal forgiveness examination
