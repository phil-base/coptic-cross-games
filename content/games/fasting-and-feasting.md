## Overview

"This kind does not go out except by prayer and fasting." (Matthew 17:21)

The Coptic Church fasts more than any other Christian tradition — over 200 days per year. But fasting is never an end in itself. Every fast prepares the heart for a feast, and every feast sends the faithful back into the world renewed. Fasting and Feasting teaches players the rhythm of the Coptic calendar, revealing the deep pattern of preparation and celebration that shapes the Orthodox life.

## How It Works

The board shows the twelve months of the Coptic calendar (Tout through Mesra, plus the small month of Nasie). Players hold **Season Cards** representing fasts, feasts, saints' commemorations, and liturgical seasons.

On each turn, a player places a card on the correct position in the calendar. Correct placements score. But the real strategy is in **linking**: placing a fast next to its corresponding feast scores bonus points (the Great Lent before the Resurrection, the Advent Fast before Nativity, the Apostles' Fast before the Feast of the Apostles). Players who can explain the connection between a fast and its feast score additional points.

The game builds the complete Coptic year piece by piece, and by the end, the group can see the whole pattern: the Church never fasts without feasting, and never feasts without having first prepared.

## What Makes It Formation

Many young Copts experience fasting as an obligation rather than a gift. This game reveals the logic behind the calendar: that fasting is preparation, not punishment, and that the feasts are more joyful because of the discipline that precedes them. The linking mechanic teaches that fasts and feasts are paired — you cannot understand one without the other.

## Components

- **Season Cards** (50) — Fasts, feasts, saints' days, and liturgical seasons
- **Board** (1 page) — The Coptic calendar wheel with twelve months
- **Rules** (2 pages) — Placement, linking, and scoring
- **Leader Guide** (6 pages) — Explanation of each fast and feast, practical fasting guidance, and discussion prompts

## Major Fasts and Feasts Include

**Fasts:**
- The Great Lent (55 days)
- Advent / Nativity Fast (43 days)
- The Apostles' Fast (15-49 days)
- The Virgin Mary's Fast (15 days)
- Wednesday and Friday weekly fasts
- The Fast of Nineveh (3 days)

**Feasts:**
- The Resurrection (Pascha)
- Nativity (7 January)
- Epiphany (Theophany)
- Feast of the Cross
- Palm Sunday, Ascension, Pentecost
- Feasts of the Virgin Mary
- Feast of the Apostles Peter and Paul
